Slot: bridge_type

Documentation: Slot that represents the bridge mounted to an electric guitar with symbols. The bridge is the piece of metal that meets the strings of a guitar at its body. Symbols: - Fixed: A non-moving bridge. This provides greater guitar tuning stability, but is not equipped with tremolo. - Standard floating: A whammy bar mounted to a bridge with springs floating it in a cavity in the back of the guitar. This is easy to use and provides tremolo, but sacrifices guitar tuning stability. - Bigsby: The first type of non-locking tremolo bridge that presses the bar to the strings rather than moving the bridge itself. - Floyd Rose: A locking variant of the standard floating bridge that includes bolts at the nut of a guitar (place where the strings meet the guitar's neck) . This provides greater tuning stability, but makes a guitar harder to restring. - Ibanez Edge: A series of modified Floyd Rose bridges that utilize an innovative knife edge design to increase tuning stability.
