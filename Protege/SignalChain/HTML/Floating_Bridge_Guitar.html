Abstract Class: Floating Bridge Guitar

Documentation: Subclass heirarchy to represent all tremolo-equipped guitars. The "bridge_type" slot of this class heirarchy is overwritten here to exclude "fixed" to reflect this. The hierarchy is broken up by locking tremolo systems and non-locking tremolo systems. A locking tremolo system is one that includes bolts at the nut of a guitar's neck to prevent the strings from sliding while using the tremolo. A non-locking tremolo system does not include these bolts.

pickup_configuration Slot representing the pickup configuration of an electric guitar with one of seven symbols. Definition: A pickup is a magnetic coil mounted to the body of an electric guitar. These coils are sensitive to changes in frequency of the guitar string, and cause electrical changes in the signal chain to produce the sound of an electric guitar. They are mounted near the bridge, neck, or halfway in between (middle). Definition: A single coil pickup a single line of magnetic coil under a string. It produces a brighter sound, but causes noise hum when it isn't played. Definition: A humbucker pickup is two single coil pickups wired out of phase. It produces a more powerful sound and "bucks" (or cancels) the hum normally experienced by single coil pickups. It has stronger harmonic response. Definition: A P90 is a classic single coil pickup designed by Gibson. P90s are less crisp than traditional single coils with extended midrange. These pickups coined the term "soapbar pickups". Symbols: - S: The guitar has one single coil pickup. - H: The guitar has one bridge humbucker. - SS: The guitar has bridge and neck single coil pickups. - HH: The guitar has a bridge and neck humbucker. - SSS: The guitar has bridge, middle, and neck single coil pickups. - HSS: The guitar has a bridge humbucker and middle and neck single coil pickups. - HSH: The guitar has bridge and neck humbuckers and a middle single coil pickup. - HHH: The guitar has bridge, middle, and neck humbucker pickups. - P90s: The guitar has classic P90 pickus. - Active: The guitar has humbuckers hooked into an active circuit. These are powered by a battery to produce even more gain on the guitar's sound and used by metal players. {S, H, SS, HH, SSS, HSS, HSH, HHH, P90s, Active} 0:1
